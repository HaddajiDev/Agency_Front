Day 1: Arrival and Acclimatization
Accommodation:

Luxury Option: Augustine Prague

Located in the Lesser Town (Malá Strana), this five-star hotel offers elegant rooms, a historic monastery setting, a spa, and fine dining.
Mid-Range Option: Hotel Neruda

Situated near Prague Castle, this boutique hotel features stylish rooms, a cozy atmosphere, and views of the city's rooftops.
Budget Option: Czech Inn

A trendy hostel in Vinohrady, offering dormitory and private rooms, a lively bar, and a communal kitchen.
Activities:

Charles Bridge: Begin your exploration with a walk across the iconic Charles Bridge, lined with statues of saints, and enjoy views of the Vltava River and Prague Castle.
Old Town Square: Visit the Old Town Square to see the Astronomical Clock, Gothic architecture, and bustling cafes.
Day 2: Exploring Historic Prague
Accommodation:

Continue your stay at your chosen hotel.
Activities:

Prague Castle: Spend the morning exploring Prague Castle, the largest ancient castle complex in the world. Visit St. Vitus Cathedral, the Old Royal Palace, and Golden Lane.
Mala Strana: Wander through the charming Lesser Town, filled with picturesque streets, baroque buildings, and hidden gardens.
Dining:

Lunch: Lokál Dlouhááá for traditional Czech cuisine and Pilsner beer.
Dinner: U Modré Kachničky for roasted duck and other Czech specialties in a cozy setting.
Day 3: Art and Culture
Accommodation:

Continue your stay at your chosen hotel.
Activities:

Jewish Quarter (Josefov): Explore the Jewish Quarter to visit the Jewish Museum, including the Old Jewish Cemetery and synagogues.
National Gallery in Prague: Spend the afternoon at the National Gallery to see Czech and international art collections, housed in beautiful historical buildings.
Dining:

Lunch: Café Savoy for a delightful brunch in a grand Art Nouveau setting.
Dinner: La Degustation Bohême Bourgeoise for a Michelin-starred dining experience with a tasting menu of Czech cuisine.
Day 4: Day Trips and Modern Prague
Accommodation:

Continue your stay at your chosen hotel.
Activities:

Kutná Hora: Take a day trip to Kutná Hora to see the UNESCO-listed Sedlec Ossuary (Bone Church) and the stunning St. Barbara's Church.
Dancing House: Return to Prague and visit the unique Dancing House, a modern architectural gem along the Vltava River.
Dining:

Lunch: Restaurace Mincovna for traditional Czech dishes in a historic setting.
Dinner: Field Restaurant for contemporary Czech cuisine with a focus on seasonal ingredients.
Day 5: Markets and Departure
Accommodation:

Check out from your hotel.
Activities:

Havelská Market: Spend your final morning exploring Havelská Market, one of Prague’s oldest markets. Shop for souvenirs, local crafts, and fresh produce.
Petřín Hill: Visit Petřín Hill for panoramic views of Prague from the top of the Petřín Tower.
Dining:

Lunch: Café Louvre for a taste of traditional Czech desserts and coffee.
Dinner: A farewell dinner at Bellevue Restaurant for fine dining with views of Prague Castle and Charles Bridge.
Tips for Your Trip
Transportation: Use Prague's efficient tram and metro system for getting around. Consider purchasing a Prague Card for unlimited public transport and museum entry.
Language: Czech is the official language, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas.
Etiquette: When dining, it’s customary to round up the bill or leave a 10% tip if service charge is not included. Greet with a handshake and use formal titles until invited to use first names.
Enjoy your trip to Prague, filled with history, art, architectural wonders, and the charm of Central Europe!
