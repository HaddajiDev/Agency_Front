Day 1: Arrival and Acclimatization
Accommodation:

Luxury Option: Four Seasons Hotel Ritz Lisbon

Located in the heart of Lisbon, this luxurious hotel offers elegant rooms, a spa, fine dining, and panoramic views over Eduardo VII Park.
Mid-Range Option: LX Boutique Hotel

Situated in the trendy neighborhood of Cais do Sodré, this boutique hotel features themed rooms inspired by Lisbon's culture, a rooftop terrace, and views of the Tagus River.
Budget Option: Goodnight Hostel

A cozy hostel in the historic district of Baixa, offering budget-friendly dormitory and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a welcoming atmosphere.
Activities:

Baixa and Chiado: Begin your Lisbon exploration by walking through Baixa, the downtown area with beautiful squares like Praça do Comércio, and Chiado, known for its elegant shops, cafes, and theaters.
Elevador de Santa Justa: Ride the Elevador de Santa Justa, a unique lift connecting Baixa to Bairro Alto, offering panoramic views of Lisbon.
Day 2: Discovering Historic Lisbon
Accommodation:

Continue your stay at your chosen hotel.
Activities:

São Jorge Castle: Explore São Jorge Castle, a Moorish fortress with stunning views over Lisbon and the Tagus River. Walk through its gardens and archaeological sites.
Alfama: Wander through Alfama, Lisbon’s oldest district, known for its narrow streets, Fado music houses, and São Vicente de Fora Church.
Dining:

Lunch: Time Out Market Lisboa for a variety of gourmet Portuguese dishes in a vibrant market setting.
Dinner: Taberna da Rua das Flores for traditional Portuguese tapas (petiscos) in a cozy atmosphere.
Day 3: Art and Culture
Accommodation:

Continue your stay at your chosen hotel.
Activities:

Belém: Take a tram or boat to Belém to visit the Jerónimos Monastery, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and taste the famous Pastéis de Belém (custard tarts) at Antiga Confeitaria de Belém.
Berardo Collection Museum: Spend the afternoon at the Berardo Collection Museum in Belém, showcasing modern and contemporary art, including works by Picasso, Warhol, and Dalí.
Dining:

Lunch: Restaurante Ponto Final for fresh seafood with views of the Tagus River in Almada.
Dinner: Bairro do Avillez for a gastronomic experience with innovative Portuguese cuisine by chef José Avillez.
Day 4: Day Trips and Modern Lisbon
Accommodation:

Continue your stay at your chosen hotel.
Activities:

Sintra: Take a day trip to Sintra to explore the fairytale-like Pena Palace, Quinta da Regaleira, and the historic town center filled with charm and beauty.
Cascais: Visit Cascais for its sandy beaches, beautiful coastline, and cultural sites like the Cascais Citadel and Paula Rego Museum.
Dining:

Lunch: Mar do Inferno in Cascais for seafood specialties with ocean views.
Dinner: Mini Bar Teatro by José Avillez for an avant-garde dining experience in Lisbon's theater district.
Day 5: Markets and Departure
Accommodation:

Check out from your hotel.
Activities:

Lisbon Cathedral: Visit Lisbon Cathedral (Sé de Lisboa) in Alfama, one of the city's oldest churches with a mix of architectural styles.
LX Factory: Explore LX Factory, a creative hub with shops, galleries, cafes, and street art under the 25 de Abril Bridge.
Dining:

Lunch: Time for a Bifana (traditional pork sandwich) at O Trevo in the historic center.
Dinner: A farewell dinner at Tágide Restaurant for Portuguese cuisine with views of the São Jorge Castle and the city lights.
Tips for Your Trip
Transportation: Use Lisbon's tram, metro, and buses for getting around. Consider purchasing a Lisboa Card for unlimited public transport and discounts on attractions.
Language: Portuguese is the official language, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas.
Etiquette: When dining, tipping around 5-10% is customary if service charge is not included. Greet with "Bom dia" (good morning) or "Boa tarde" (good afternoon).
Enjoy your trip to Lisbon, filled with history, culture, delicious cuisine, and the laid-back charm of Portugal's capital city!
