Day 1: Arrival and Acclimatization
Accommodation:

Luxury Option: Hotel Eden

Located near the Spanish Steps and Villa Borghese, this five-star hotel offers opulent rooms, a rooftop restaurant with panoramic views, and a luxurious spa.
Mid-Range Option: Hotel Artemide

Centrally located on Via Nazionale, this hotel offers modern rooms, a rooftop terrace, and excellent amenities including a wellness center.
Budget Option: Hotel Santa Maria

Situated in the charming Trastevere district, this budget-friendly hotel offers cozy rooms, a lovely garden, and easy access to local eateries and attractions.
Activities:

Spanish Steps: Begin your trip with a visit to the iconic Spanish Steps. Climb to the top for a view of the bustling Piazza di Spagna.
Trevi Fountain: Toss a coin into the Trevi Fountain to ensure your return to Rome. Admire the stunning Baroque architecture.
Day 2: Exploring Ancient Rome
Accommodation:

Continue your stay at your chosen hotel.
Activities:

Colosseum: Spend the morning exploring the Colosseum, the largest amphitheater ever built. Consider a guided tour to learn about its fascinating history.
Roman Forum: Walk through the ancient ruins of the Roman Forum, once the heart of Roman public life. Highlights include the Temple of Saturn and the Arch of Titus.
Palatine Hill: Visit Palatine Hill for breathtaking views of the city and a glimpse into Rome’s imperial past.
Dining:

Lunch: Hostaria da Nerone near the Colosseum for authentic Roman cuisine.
Dinner: Trattoria da Enzo al 29 in Trastevere for traditional dishes in a cozy setting.
Day 3: Art and Culture
Accommodation:

Continue your stay at your chosen hotel.
Activities:

Vatican Museums: Start your day at the Vatican Museums. Don’t miss the Sistine Chapel and Raphael Rooms.
St. Peter’s Basilica: Visit the stunning St. Peter’s Basilica. Climb to the top of the dome for a panoramic view of Vatican City.
Castel Sant’Angelo: Explore this historic fortress, which offers spectacular views of the Tiber River and the Vatican.
Dining:

Lunch: Pizzarium Bonci near the Vatican for some of the best pizza in Rome.
Dinner: Roscioli for an exquisite Italian meal with a focus on high-quality ingredients.
Day 4: Hidden Gems and Relaxation
Accommodation:

Continue your stay at your chosen hotel.
Activities:

Borghese Gallery: Spend your morning at the Borghese Gallery, home to a remarkable collection of sculptures and paintings by masters like Bernini and Caravaggio.
Villa Borghese Gardens: Take a leisurely stroll or rent a bike to explore the beautiful gardens surrounding the gallery.
Trastevere: Wander through the picturesque streets of Trastevere, known for its vibrant nightlife and charming atmosphere.
Dining:

Lunch: Osteria der Belli in Trastevere for fresh seafood and Roman specialties.
Dinner: La Pergola, Rome’s only three-Michelin-starred restaurant, for a luxurious dining experience.
Day 5: Day Trip and Departure
Accommodation:

Check out from your hotel.
Activities:

Day Trip to Tivoli: Visit Villa d’Este and Hadrian’s Villa in Tivoli, both UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Marvel at the Renaissance architecture and stunning gardens.
Last-minute Shopping: Return to Rome for some last-minute shopping along Via del Corso or in the elegant boutiques around Piazza di Spagna.
Dining:

Lunch: Ristorante Sibilla in Tivoli for a meal with a view.
Dinner: A farewell dinner at Armando al Pantheon, offering classic Roman dishes in a historic setting.
Tips for Your Trip
Transportation: Use Rome’s Metro and bus system for convenient travel around the city. Consider purchasing a Roma Pass for unlimited public transport and discounts on museum entries.
Language: Italian is the primary language spoken, but many locals speak English, especially in tourist areas.
Etiquette: Be mindful of local customs, such as dressing modestly when visiting churches and greeting people with "Buongiorno" or "Buonasera."
Enjoy your trip to Rome, filled with ancient history, magnificent art, and the vibrant culture of the Eternal City!
