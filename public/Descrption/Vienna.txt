Day 1: Arrival and Acclimatization
Accommodation:

Luxury Option: Hotel Sacher Wien

Located near the Vienna State Opera, this luxurious hotel offers elegant rooms, a renowned café for Sachertorte, spa facilities, and impeccable service.
Mid-Range Option: Hotel Am Konzerthaus Vienna - MGallery

Situated in the heart of the city, this hotel features stylish rooms, a baroque-style inner courtyard, and is close to Belvedere Palace and Vienna's concert halls.
Budget Option: Meininger Hotel Vienna Central Station

A modern hostel near the main train station, offering budget-friendly dormitory and private rooms, a guest kitchen, and a lounge area.
Activities:

St. Stephen's Cathedral: Begin your Vienna exploration with a visit to St. Stephen's Cathedral (Stephansdom), an iconic symbol of Vienna with its stunning Gothic architecture and panoramic views from the tower.
Vienna State Opera: Admire the Vienna State Opera from the outside and consider attending a performance or taking a guided tour of this prestigious venue.
Day 2: Discovering Imperial Vienna
Accommodation:

Continue your stay at your chosen hotel.
Activities:

Schönbrunn Palace: Explore Schönbrunn Palace, the former imperial summer residence with its lavish interiors, Baroque gardens, and the Gloriette offering scenic views over Vienna.
Hofburg Palace: Visit Hofburg Palace, the winter residence of the Habsburgs, now home to the Imperial Apartments, the Sisi Museum, and the Spanish Riding School.
Dining:

Lunch: Café Central for a taste of Viennese coffeehouse culture and classic Austrian dishes.
Dinner: Plachutta Wollzeile for traditional Viennese cuisine, especially their famous Tafelspitz (boiled beef).
Day 3: Art and Culture
Accommodation:

Continue your stay at your chosen hotel.
Activities:

Museums Quartier: Spend the morning at Museums Quartier, one of the largest cultural complexes in the world, housing museums like the Leopold Museum (featuring Egon Schiele) and the Museum of Modern Art (MUMOK).
Belvedere Palace: Visit Belvedere Palace to see its impressive art collection, including Gustav Klimt's "The Kiss," and stroll through its landscaped gardens.
Dining:

Lunch: Naschmarkt for a diverse selection of international and Austrian cuisine at Vienna's largest outdoor market.
Dinner: Steirereck im Stadtpark for a fine dining experience with innovative Austrian cuisine and a beautiful park setting.
Day 4: Day Trips and Modern Vienna
Accommodation:

Continue your stay at your chosen hotel.
Activities:

Danube River Cruise: Take a leisurely Danube River cruise to enjoy views of Vienna's skyline and nearby vineyards.
Prater Park: Visit Prater Park to ride the historic Ferris wheel (Riesenrad), one of Vienna’s symbols, and explore the amusement park and green spaces.
Dining:

Lunch: Gasthaus Pöschl for traditional Austrian dishes in a cozy tavern setting.
Dinner: Fabios for contemporary Italian-Austrian fusion cuisine in a chic atmosphere.
Day 5: Markets and Departure
Accommodation:

Check out from your hotel.
Activities:

Naschmarkt: Spend your final morning exploring Naschmarkt again, perhaps picking up souvenirs or enjoying brunch at one of its many eateries.
Vienna State Library: Visit the Austrian National Library (Österreichische Nationalbibliothek) to admire its stunning Baroque architecture and impressive collection of books and manuscripts.
Dining:

Lunch: Figlmueller for Wiener Schnitzel, a classic Austrian dish, in a historic restaurant near St. Stephen's Cathedral.
Dinner: A farewell dinner at Do & Co Restaurant for rooftop dining with panoramic views of St. Stephen's Cathedral and the city lights.
Tips for Your Trip
Transportation: Use Vienna's efficient U-Bahn (subway), tram, and bus systems. Consider purchasing a Vienna City Card for unlimited public transport and discounts on attractions.
Language: German is the official language, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas.
Etiquette: When dining, it’s customary to round up the bill or leave a 5-10% tip if service charge is not included. Greet with "Guten Tag" (good day) or "Guten Abend" (good evening).
Enjoy your trip to Vienna, filled with imperial history, world-class art, classical music, and the charm of Austria's capital city!
